# ARCANA
Regime-Aware Statistical Arbitrage: Rolling Cointegration with Dynamic Volatility Targeting in the S&amp;P 100
This project presents ARCANA, a regime-aware statistical arbitrage framework applied to the S&P 100 universe over the period January 2013 to December 2025. The strategy combines a three-year rolling cointegration procedure on log prices, restricted to economically related pairs within the same GICS sector, with a Hidden Markov Model that classifies the market into three distinct states: Bull, Neutral, and Crisis. Position sizes are scaled dynamically in proportion to regime confidence, and a volatility targeting overlay maintains consistent portfolio risk through changing market conditions. Across 13 calendar years, the strategy delivers a net annualised return of 17.35 percent at 6.85 percent volatility, producing a Sharpe ratio of 2.534 after realistic transaction costs of 5 to 15 basis points per side. The Newey-West corrected t-statistic of 9.454 confirms statistical significance at the 1 percent level, and a Durbin-Watson statistic of 2.012 confirms that the Sharpe ratio is not inflated by return autocorrelation. Out-of-sample performance over the period 2021 to 2025 produces a Sharpe ratio of 2.998, which exceeds the in-sample estimate of 2.722, with an alpha decay of 29.1 percent, well within conventional robustness thresholds. The strategy generates positive returns in all 13 calendar years, including the 2020 COVID-19 shock and the 2022 interest rate crisis, and maintains a portfolio beta of approximately 0.009.
